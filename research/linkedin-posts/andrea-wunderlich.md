### Post 2
- Date: 1 month ago
- Access Method: manual screenshot capture
- Topic: Why GTM should be organized around the customer’s job-to-be-done rather than around launches, sprints, and internal release cycles.
- Summary: Andrea argues that companies often mistake internal product activity for market progress. She contrasts launches and releases, which are internal events, with JTBDs, which reflect what customers are actually trying to stabilize or improve in their world. She explains that when teams organize around launches, every feature starts to look strategic, but when they organize around JTBD, the standard becomes whether the company is deepening its ability to solve the core customer problem. The post links JTBD directly to use cases, capabilities, roadmap choices, and buyer incentives.
- Key Insight: GTM becomes fragmented when it is anchored to internal launch activity instead of the external customer job that gives the market coherence.
- Relevance to B2B SaaS LinkedIn organic strategy: Very high because it gives a reusable strategic framework for positioning, content planning, market education, and demand messaging.
- Reusable Takeaway: Strong B2B LinkedIn content should anchor messaging in the customer’s real job-to-be-done, not in internal company milestones.
